Career

national team career

Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese football player who has had a successful career representing the Portugal national team. Ronaldo made his debut on the international stage for Portugal's U-17 team in 2003, and he subsequently played for the U-20 and U-21 teams.

In 2004, he caught the attention of then Portuguese national team coach Luiz Felipe Scolari and was included in the roster for the European Championship. This provided Ronaldo with the opportunity to make his first appearance for the national team at the age of 19.

Since then, Ronaldo has established himself as a key player for the Portuguese national team, participating in various international competitions. He has represented Portugal in the 2006 World Cup in Germany, Euro 2008, 2010 World Cup in South Africa, Euro 2012, 2014 World Cup in Brazil, Euro 2016, 2018 World Cup in Russia, and Euro 2020 in 2021.

Ronaldo has displayed exceptional performance for the Portuguese national team, contributing with important goals. His offensive abilities and leadership have greatly benefited the team, allowing Portugal to perform at a high level in international competitions.

Ronaldo achieved remarkable success with the national team by leading Portugal to victory in Euro 2016. In that tournament, he scored a goal in the final and was named the MVP of the final. Additionally, Ronaldo has received numerous individual honors, and his contributions and achievements have been widely recognized by the Portuguese national team.

Overall, Ronaldo has played a significant number of matches for the Portuguese national team and has been a key player internationally. His national team career has been an integral part of showcasing his exceptional talent and skills as a football player.

club career

Sporting Lisbon (2002-2003): Ronaldo began his professional career at Sporting Lisbon in Portugal. He made his first-team debut in 2002 at the age of 17. His impressive performances caught the attention of Manchester United scouts.

Manchester United (2003-2009): In 2003, Ronaldo signed with Manchester United in the English Premier League. Under the guidance of manager Sir Alex Ferguson, Ronaldo flourished and became one of the world's best players. During his time at Manchester United, Ronaldo won numerous trophies, including three Premier League titles and the UEFA Champions League in 2008. He also won his first Ballon d'Or award in 2008.

Real Madrid (2009-2018): In 2009, Ronaldo made a high-profile move to Real Madrid, becoming the most expensive player at the time. At Real Madrid, he reached new heights and solidified his status as one of the greatest players in history. Ronaldo's tenure at Real Madrid was marked by extraordinary goal-scoring exploits, winning four Champions League titles and two La Liga titles. He also won the Ballon d'Or four times during his time at the club, bringing his total to five.

Juventus (2018-2021): In 2018, Ronaldo joined Juventus in Serie A, the top professional league in Italy. He continued to showcase his exceptional skills and goal-scoring ability, helping Juventus win two Serie A titles during his time with the club. Ronaldo's stint at Juventus further demonstrated his versatility and longevity as a player.

Return to Manchester United (2021-present): In August 2021, Ronaldo made a highly anticipated return to Manchester United, rejoining the club where he made his mark on the world stage. His return has been met with immense excitement and anticipation from fans. Ronaldo's return to Manchester United provides him with an opportunity to add to his already illustrious club career.
